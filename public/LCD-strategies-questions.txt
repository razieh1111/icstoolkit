## 1.1
- How can the product's material composition be optimized for reduced environmental impact?
- Are there opportunities to use recycled or renewable materials?

## 1.2
- Can the product's manufacturing processes be made more energy-efficient?
- Are there ways to reduce waste generation during production?

## 1.3
- How can the product's distribution and logistics be optimized to lower emissions?
- Can local sourcing or production reduce transportation impacts?

## 1.4
- What is the product's expected lifespan, and how can it be extended?
- Are components designed for easy repair, upgrade, or replacement?

## 1.5
- How can the product's energy consumption during use be minimized?
- Are there smart features or user behaviors that can reduce energy use?

## 2.1
- Can the product be easily disassembled at the end of its life?
- Are materials clearly identified for sorting and recycling?

## 2.2
- Are there established recycling or composting infrastructures for the product's materials?
- Can the product be designed to use fewer different types of materials?

## 2.3
- Can the product be designed for reuse in its entirety or as components?
- Are there take-back schemes or secondary markets for the product?

## 3.1
- How can the product's design minimize the use of hazardous substances?
- Are there safer alternatives for materials or processes?

## 3.2
- Does the product contribute to indoor or outdoor air pollution?
- How can emissions to air, water, and soil be reduced?

## 4.1
- How can the product's design reduce its overall weight?
- Can material intensity be reduced without compromising performance?

## 4.2
- Are there opportunities to use fewer components or simplify the product structure?
- Can multi-functional components replace several single-function ones?

## 5
- How can the product be designed to be more durable and robust?
- What features can prevent premature obsolescence?

## 6
- How can the product's design facilitate maintenance and repair by users or professionals?
- Are spare parts readily available and affordable?

## 7.1
- How can the product's design encourage emotional attachment and user satisfaction?
- Does the product offer a unique aesthetic or user experience?

## 7.2
- Can the product be designed for adaptability to changing user needs or contexts?

## 7.3
- How can the product's design promote a sense of community or shared ownership?
- Does it facilitate collaborative use or sharing models?

## 7.4
- Can the product's design integrate natural elements or biomimicry?

## 7.5
- How can the product's design educate users about its environmental impact?

## 7.6
- Can the product's design support local economies and craftsmanship?

## 7.7
- How can the product's design promote ethical sourcing and fair labor practices?

## 7.8
